About SHRC

A Legacy of Rehabilitation in Lebanon

Founded in 1990 by Dr. Mohamad Halimi, the Saad Halimi Rehabilitation Center has served patients across Tripoli and North Lebanon for over three decades.

Our purpose is to help you continue participating in your daily activities and life roles. We design treatment programs based on both the findings of our evaluation and your personal goals — because movement and function matter to every aspect of your life.

We provide therapeutic treatment wherever movement and function are threatened by aging, injury, disease, or environmental factors, with one goal: improving your quality of life.

SHRC is fully equipped with state-of-the-art rehabilitation and preventive technology, combining Western evidence-based medicine with effective Eastern therapeutic traditions for a truly integrative approach to care.

Isokinetic Therapy
Using the HUMAC® NORM™ system, we quantify muscular deficits before and after treatment — designing precise rehabilitation programs and objectively measuring your recovery.

About the technology
The HUMAC® NORM™ by CSMi is the gold standard in isokinetic evaluation and rehabilitation, trusted by major hospitals including Massachusetts General Hospital. It measures the force your muscles produce through a full range of motion — identifying exactly where weakness or imbalance exists. This data guides your entire treatment plan and objectively proves your progress at discharge.

Chinese & Japanese Acupuncture
Acupuncture increases the body's release of natural painkillers in the pain pathways of both the spinal cord and brain — modifying the way pain signals are received. Osteopathy

Holistic body treatment
Osteopathy observes and treats the entire body as a connected system. Spinal blockages cause muscle tension, headaches, shoulder and back pain. Vertebral restrictions can lead to digestive disorders and heartburn. Pelvic torsion causes menstrual and bladder dysfunction. Manipulation uses measured movements of varying speed, force, and distance to reposition joints — reducing pain and restoring flexibility. Medical Thermography
Infrared imaging that visualizes pain and disease quickly and non-invasively. A powerful diagnostic tool that identifies the root cause of your symptoms before treatment begins.
Photo coming soon
Visualize pain with infrared imaging
Medical Thermography is an infrared imaging diagnostic technology capable of identifying pain and disease quickly and safely — without radiation. It detects temperature differences on the body surface caused by inflammation, nerve damage, or vascular issues. Think of it as making the invisible visible: you can literally see where pain originates before treatment starts, allowing for a more targeted and effective recovery plan.

Ergonomy
We evaluate your workplace, sleep habits, and daily activities to identify risk factors and recommend ergonomic solutions — preventing musculoskeletal problems before they develop.
Photo coming soon
Prevention before treatment
Primary Prevention: we evaluate situations presenting risk factors for musculoskeletal pathologies — at work, at home, during sleep — and propose ergonomic tools and posture corrections. This keeps our clients from becoming patients. Secondary Prevention: once a pathology exists, we manage altered structures with ergonomic products adapted to the patient's specific situation — preventing relapse. Products cover: workplace (chair, desk, computer), sleeping position, daily activities, and environment (lighting, air conditioning).

Women's Health

Pelvic floor rehabilitation
The rehabilitation of the pelvic muscles is essential to prevent or treat involuntary urine leakage caused by laughing, coughing, or exercise. We also treat feelings of heaviness or pressure in the lower abdomen (signs of uterine or bladder prolapse), pain during sexual intercourse (dyspareunia or vaginismus), and back pains synchronized with the menstrual cycle. Our role is to strengthen the pelvic muscles, improve their effectiveness, control leakage, and relieve pressure and pain — in a private, respectful setting.

Traditional cupping therapy
